## A1: Modern Newspaper

**Best for:** Business reports, economic media, research findings, data-heavy analysis
**Mood:** Intellectual, smartphone-generation business media, high-energy editorial
**Colors:** White/Cool Gray bg, Jet Black text, Electric Yellow or Alert Red accent

```
You are a top art director leading Japan's "new economy business media." But the language should be what users said in the prompt.
Based on the following "design definition," generate a visually focused, high-sensibility presentation slide that sparks intellectual excitement in business professionals of the smartphone generation.

[Important: Absolutely Prohibited Output Format Rules]
* **Complete Exclusion of Markdown Symbols**: Do not include symbols like "#" for headings or "*" and "**" for emphasis in the slide text **under any circumstances**.
* **Plain Text Only**: Text displayed on the slide must consist solely of "pure (the language what users requested in the prompt) and English text" without any decorative symbols.

[Special Specification for Cover Slide: Make This the Highest Quality]
* **Design Philosophy**: Draw inspiration from "Swiss Style (International Typographic Style)" or "Bauhaus."
* **Layout**: Ban simplistic "centered alignment." Create tension with **"asymmetrical"** placement. Use a grid system to position the title extremely off to the top left or bottom left, or craft bold negative space for refinement.
* **Title Copy Design**:
    * **Main Title (Ultra-Large · Short Phrase)**: Ditch descriptive words. Make it a visual anchor with a short word or phrase of about "2–5 characters" (e.g., "Liberation," "Collapse and Rebirth," "The True Face of AI").
    * **Subtitle (Ultra-Small · Benefit-Driven)**: Carve into the reader's pain points and hint at resolution with a concise sentence (e.g., "Why Is Your Advertising Dead?" "How to Ditch 'Tasks' and Return to 'Creation'").
    * **Composition Ratio**: Punch the eyes with the main title, stab the brain with the subtitle.

[Overall Design Definition for All Slides]
1.  **Core Theme**: Smart & pop business infotainment (intellectual curiosity × entertainment)
2.  **Color Palette**:
    * Background Color: White (#FFFFFF) or Cool Gray (#F5F5F5)
    * Text Color: Sumi (Jet Black) (#111111)
    * Accent Color: Electric Yellow (#FFCC00) or Alert Red (#FF3333)
3.  **Visual Style**:
    * Adopt the design philosophy of a "**smartphone-first economic media**."
    * Use images like "monochrome cutouts of people" or "stylish photos with blown-out backgrounds" to emphasize the subject.
    * Highlight key numbers or keywords with fluorescent marker-style lines (yellow background) to create rhythm.

4.  **Typography (Text as Graphic)**:
    * Position headlines at an "**ultra-massive size**" occupying **30%–50%** of the slide's area.
    * **Extreme Jump Ratio**: The size ratio between "headlines" and "body text" must be **10:1** or more. No half-hearted size differences allowed.
    * For headlines, use **extra-bold sans-serif** (Impact/(Hiragino) W8 weight) to treat them as a "surface," and tuck ultra-thin English fonts into the gaps for a sense of airiness.

5.  **Overall Structure**:
    * Strictly adhere to "1 slide = 1 message."
    * Layout is a binary choice between "negative space" or "text." Draw the eye through contrasts of text-packed areas that fill the screen and vast empty voids.
    * Place the conclusion (punchline) with a "bam!" in the center of the slide, or position it spilling off the edge for visual impact.
```

## A2: Sharp-Edged Minimalism

**Best for:** Portfolio, architecture, premium brand decks, consulting, agency work
**Mood:** Architectural, refined, professional, luxury whitespace
**Colors:** Light Gray or White bg, Jet/Dark Gray text, Black accent

```
# presentation_design_spec_minimal_jp.yaml
# Style: Refined Minimal Portfolio
# Characteristics: Top-left navigation, aesthetics of whitespace, grid-based layout

Global Design Settings:
  Tone: "Professional, architectural, sharp-edged minimalism"
  Color Palette:
    Base: "#E9E9E9 (light gray) or #FFFFFF (white)"
    Text: "#000000 (jet black) or #333333 (dark gray)"
    Accent: "#000000 (black) – used for bold lines and emphasized text"
    Special: "Dark mode (black background) – used for slides that need emphasis"
  Typography:
    Headings: "English sans-serif (e.g., Helvetica Now, Inter). Bold and decoratively positioned."
    Body: "(the language what users requested in the prompt) Gothic typeface. Small size with generous letter spacing and line height."
  Common Layout Rules:
    Navigation: "Place a small section number and title such as '01. INTRODUCTION' in the top-left (or top-right) of every slide."
    Grid: "Use a strict grid system to align elements."
    Whitespace: "Intentionally leave large areas empty (negative space) to create a sense of luxury."

Layout Variations (Catalog):
  - Type: "Title Typography" / Design: "Scattered layout. Randomly place award badges or keywords like stamps."
  - Type: "Text + Data Emphasis" / Design: "Asymmetrical split. Narrative text on the left, oversized numbers (black) on the right. Include thin divider lines."
  - Type: "Card Grid" / Design: "Tightly spaced grid. High-quality images, web-like interaction feel."
  - Type: "Full-Screen Graphic" / Design: "Office/architectural photography full screen. Reduce saturation. Small caption bottom-left."
  - Type: "Photo + List Split" / Design: "50:50 split. Left: architectural photo. Right: data list (bold headings + light descriptions)."
  - Type: "Minimal Map" / Design: "Silhouette-style map. Light gray bg, white map, ultra-thin callout lines."
  - Type: "Vertical Timeline" / Design: "Single thin line, text branching left and right. Clean chronological order."
  - Type: "Bubble Chart / Venn" / Design: "Wireframe style. Black bg, thin white line art. Semi-transparent overlapping circles."
  - Type: "Dark Mode Diagram" / Design: "Black bg, thin white lines connecting nodes. Constellation/network-like."
  - Type: "3-Step Columns" / Design: "Large numbers (01, 02, 03) as pillars. No icons; typographic contrast only."
  - Type: "Two Columns (Problem vs Solution)" / Design: "Thick black vertical divider. Text aligned in block form."
  - Type: "Formula / Flow" / Design: "Mathematical style. 'A × B = C' in large serif type. Minimal arrows."

## B2: Magazine Style

**Best for:** Consumer content, lifestyle brands, beauty/wellness, product features
**Mood:** Mature-cute, sophisticated, feminine, trendy, polished
**Colors:** Dusty/shell pink bg, charcoal/black text, white accents

```
# Instructions
Please compile [Source Information] in a [mature-cute, sophisticated magazine-style editorial design].

# Layout Requirements
・Place a large cutout photo of the subject (model woman or product) in the center, emphasizing movement or pose
・Place speech bubbles or text boxes asymmetrically in the left and right margins
・Distribute main points as small sections numbered like "NO.1" "NO.2"
・Place a vertical text box (white background) at the edge of the screen (e.g., right side) and insert a catchy copy
・Place L-shaped lines resembling crop marks ("trim marks") in the four corners to create a poster or page-like feel
・Format: Landscape (4:3 or 3:2), high resolution
・But the language should be what users said in the prompt.

# Design Requirements
・Background color: Matte, subdued tone stylish pink (dusty pink or shell pink)
・Main image: Use a photo with the background completely removed (deep etching)
・Decorative elements: Use hand-drawn style speech bubbles, simple circles, and thin straight lines as accents
・Fonts: Headings in sophisticated Gothic or Mincho, comments in hand-drawn style font for a casual feel
・In speech bubbles or text boxes, write key points from the source information in emotional handwritten text of a young woman
・Color palette: Matte pink for background, charcoal gray or black for text, white for accents
・Overall atmosphere: Mature-cute, feminine, trendy, polished tone

# Expressions to Avoid
・Overly childish vivid colors, overly flashy gradients, heavy shadows
```

## D5: Anti-Gravity / Living Artifact

**Best for:** AI products, agent systems, tech infrastructure launches, research decks
**Mood:** Apple-level clarity, calm, inevitable, systemic, minimal
**Colors:** Pure white bg, soft blue/cyan/violet gradient accents (corners only), dark gray text, calm blue accent

```
# Style: Anti-Gravity / Living Artifact Presentation
# Concept: "This system already works. We are just showing you."

Core Concept:
  This presentation is not a slide deck. It is a living artifact.
  Feels like: Apple-level clarity + Google DeepMind research decks + Calm AI infrastructure product launches.

Background & Canvas:
  Pure white background as default.
  Add soft flowing gradient accents: Blue → cyan → violet, very low opacity, corners/edges only. Never behind text.

Typography:
  Headlines: Clean modern sans-serif, slightly rounded geometry, medium-bold weight, calm authority.
  Hierarchy: Large headline → one concise explanatory sentence → short bullet/paragraph blocks. No long paragraphs.

Color System:
  Primary text: Black or very dark gray.
  Accent: Calm blue (used sparingly).
  Gradients: Decorative only, not structural.

Layout Language:
  Left-aligned layouts. Clear reading flow. Wide margins. Lots of white space.
  Common structures: Text left / visual right. Three-column feature cards. One idea per slide.

Visual Metaphors:
  A. Thought → Structure: Messy scribble → arrow → clean diagram/checklist/UI
  B. Interface as Proof: Realistic browser/app screenshots. Cursor highlights. Click indicators.
  C. Cards as Capabilities: Soft rounded rectangles. Subtle shadows. Icon + title + 1-2 lines.

Iconography: Thin-line icons. Outline style. Consistent stroke weight. Professional. No pixel art. No emojis.

What to AVOID: No pixel art. No thick borders. No bright blocks. No collage. No stickers. No loud contrasts.
  Rule: If it feels "fun", it's wrong. If it feels "inevitable", it's right.
```

## D6: Neo-Retro Dev Deck

**Best for:** Developer tools, coding products, startup pitch to builders, AI tools
**Mood:** Retro-futuristic, developer-centric, editorial infographic, slightly rebellious
**Colors:** Cream/off-white grid bg, hot pink / bright yellow / cyan color blocks, black outlines

```
# Style: Neo-Retro Dev Deck / Pixel-Infographic Editorial

Core Aesthetic: Retro-futuristic, developer-centric. Feels like: 90s computer manuals + modern AI dev tools marketing + pixel-art meets startup slide deck.

Canvas: Light cream/off-white grid paper background. Subtle square grid (engineering notebook feel). Slides = annotated engineering notes.

Typography:
  Primary headline: Bold heavy sans-serif, slightly condensed, high contrast.
  Language mixing: Primary language + English mixed. English sub-labels under titles.
  Hierarchy: Huge bold headline → medium sub-titles → small explanatory captions.

Color Palette (Strict): High-contrast blocks with thick black outlines.
  Hot Pink — agent/brain/intelligence concepts
  Bright Yellow — editor/code/tools
  Cyan/Light Blue — browser/web/execution
  Black — text, borders
  White/cream — background
  Each section = one dominant color block.

Layout: Stacked modular blocks. Rectangles with thick black borders. Slight overlaps allowed (controlled imperfection). Cards for steps, evolution, layers.

Iconography: Pixel-art style icons. 8-bit or 16-bit inspired. Flat colors, black outline.
  Examples: Rocket, Robot/agent, Gear, Code brackets, Browser window, Chat bubbles.
  Decorative: Small gears, arrows, chevrons <>, pixel sparks/motion lines.

Content Patterns:
  A. "System Architecture": Stacked layers with labels. Each layer: one color + one icon + one bold title + one short line.
  B. "Evolution / Timeline": Left→right progression. Each step in its own box. Final step larger and more colorful.
  C. "Manifesto / Thesis": One huge headline in boxed frame. Minimal text. Surrounded by playful icons. Feels like a statement.

Tone of Text: Short. Declarative. Slightly opinionated. No marketing fluff.

What to AVOID: No gradients. No realistic photos. No soft shadows. No corporate templates. No minimalism for its own sake.
```

## G1: Blueprint Technical Manual

**Best for:** Software documentation, technical education, engineering explainers, product teardowns, developer-facing content
**Mood:** Precise, nostalgic, intellectual, handcrafted-technical
**Colors:** Blueprint navy background (#0A1628 or white variant), electric cobalt blue (#1E5EFF) as primary ink, white (#FFFFFF) for labels, light blue wash (#C8D8F5) for shaded fills

```
You are a top art director creating technical documentation with a blueprint aesthetic. The language for all slide text should be what users said in the prompt.

Global Design Settings:
  Tone: "Technical drafting manual meets retro computing aesthetic — obsessively precise yet warmly illustrated"
  Color Palette:
    Background: "#F5F7FF (blueprint paper white) or #0D1B3E (dark blueprint variant)"
    Primary Ink / Text: "#1A4FCC (blueprint cobalt)"
    Body Text: "#1A1A2E (near-black navy)"
    Accent Fill: "#A8C0F0 (translucent blue wash for 3D form shading)"
    Grid/Rule Lines: "#C5D3EE (subtle, low-contrast)"
    Labels/Callouts: "#0033BB bold on white, or white on dark"
  Typography:
    Display/Title: "Pixel or bitmap-style monospace — reminiscent of early dot-matrix printing. All caps, wide tracking (+200–400). Reference fonts: Share Tech Mono, VT323, Courier Prime Bold. Size ratio: ~3–4× body."
    Body Copy: "Classic serif with drop cap on first paragraph — Georgia, EB Garamond, or Playfair Display. Justified alignment. 14–16px, tight leading (1.3–1.4)."
    Labels/Callouts: "Clean monospace caps — IBM Plex Mono, Roboto Mono. Uppercase, small (10–12px), tracked out."
    Figure Numbers: "Rotated 90° vertically along left margin, tiny monospace. E.g., 'FIG.001'"
  Visual Style:
    - All illustrations are isometric or exploded-view technical line drawings — blueprint style, rendered in single cobalt blue with light blue fill for depth
    - Diagrams use dashed leader lines with arrowheads pointing to labeled components
    - Grid of fine dots or graph lines overlaid on background at very low opacity (~8–12%)
    - Thin rule borders around content zones (hairline, 0.5–1px)
    - "FIG.00X" figure numbering rotated vertically in the left margin of each diagram
    - Bracket notations [ LABEL ] used for section identifiers in margins
    - No photography. No gradients. No drop shadows. Flat technical ink aesthetic only.

Layout Variations:
  - Type: "Title Slide" / Design: "Pixel/bitmap display headline in cobalt blue top-left, spanning ~50% width. Subtitle and author info right-aligned top-right in serif italic. Full-width dotted rule separating header. Remaining canvas split: left = body text column, right = large featured technical diagram with callout lines."
  - Type: "Concept Explainer" / Design: "Two-column — left column contains justified body copy with drop caps; right column has isometric diagram with leader-line labels. Figure number rotated vertically in far-left margin."
  - Type: "Diagram / Teardown" / Design: "Near-full-bleed exploded-view illustration centered on right 60% of slide. Component labels in monospace caps connected via dashed lines. Title in small pixel font top-left. Minimal body text."
